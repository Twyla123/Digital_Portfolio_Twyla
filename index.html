About Me

I’m Xinxin Zhang, a graduate student in Data Science at Columbia University and a recent Economics and Data Science graduate from the University of Southern California. My research focuses on applying data science to understand real-world economic and social issues, using programming and analytics to uncover meaningful patterns behind policy and technology. I’ve conducted research on topics such as artificial intelligence and labor-market inequality, financial stability, and rural healthcare systems. Across all my projects, I focus on transforming complex datasets into clear, actionable insights. I’m passionate about applying data science to address social and economic challenges and developing transparent, data-driven solutions that make a meaningful impact.
